Payments architecture draft

Separate SaaS Revenue From Parent Tuition Money

The clean structure is two payment systems under one billing module: subscriptions for access to The BEE Suite, and tuition/payment collection for schools and parents.

1. Kid City USA corporate subscription

Corporate pays one recurring monthly amount that covers all Kid City USA locations.

Kid City corporateMaster customer record and billing contact.
Stripe BillingMonthly subscription, invoice, dunning, payment method updates.
The BEE SuiteAll Kid City locations remain active under the covered corporate plan.

2. Independent school subscription

Single-location or small multi-location schools enter payment details during onboarding.

School ownerChooses plan and enters billing info during onboarding.
Checkout + BillingCreates subscription, saves payment method, manages renewals.
Tenant accessPlan controls active locations, users, feature flags, and billing status.

3. Parent tuition payments

Parent tuition should not be mixed with SaaS subscription revenue.

Parent portalACH preferred, card optional, fee disclosure before payment.
Stripe ConnectSchool connected account receives tuition funds; optional platform fee if approved.
School ledgerInvoice, payment, refund, balance, and payout reconciliation stay school-scoped.

Recommended default model

Use Stripe Billing for BEE Suite subscriptions. Use Stripe Connect for tuition, with each school as the merchant/connected account. Make ACH the default parent payment path and make card payments optional with compliant fee recovery language.

SaaS access fee

Cleanest

  • Corporate or school pays The BEE Suite.
  • Use subscription products and prices.
  • No parent tuition funds involved.

Tuition ACH

Lowest cost

  • Preferred default for recurring tuition.
  • Lower processing cost than cards.
  • Best way to compete with Tuition Express.

Tuition card

Optional

  • Convenient but expensive.
  • Use clear parent disclosure.
  • Debit/prepaid and card recovery rules need review.

Fee strategy decision tree

If parent pays ACH

Charge no parent-paid processing recovery unless the school policy and legal/accounting review approve it. Platform margin should mostly come from SaaS subscription, not tuition markup.

If parent pays card

Show a disclosed processing recovery line before confirmation. Keep it at or below the Procare/Tuition Express benchmark once verified.

If school absorbs fees

Parent sees a cleaner experience, but the school bears card costs. Offer ACH autopay nudges and reporting to reduce card usage.

If platform takes margin

Use an approved platform/application fee only where the school has opted in. Track it separately from tuition and processor fees.

What we still need before building production tuition payments

  • Actual Kid City / Procare Tuition Express merchant statement or fee schedule for ACH, credit, debit, returns, chargebacks, and funding speed.
  • Decision on whether each school or The BEE Suite is merchant of record for tuition. Recommendation: school is merchant/connected account.
  • Legal/accounting review for payment processing recovery, debit/prepaid handling, state rules, parent authorization, ACH returns, refunds, disputes, and disclosures.
  • Final policy: ACH default, card optional, school absorbs vs parent pays vs mixed by school setting.
Pricing reference: Stripe public pricing as checked on June 8, 2026 lists domestic cards at 2.9% + 30c and ACH Direct Debit at 0.8% with a $5 cap. Stripe Billing pay-as-you-go is listed at 0.7% of Billing volume. Procare public docs describe Tuition Express funding/reporting and merchant statements, but do not publish a complete current fee schedule.
