# Privacy Policy Draft - The BEE Suite

Draft date: July 9, 2026  
Status: Draft for owner and counsel review. Do not publish without legal approval.  
Suggested public URL: `https://thebeesuite.io/privacy`

## 1. Overview

The BEE Suite is a childcare operations platform used by childcare centers, schools, owner groups, staff, parents, guardians, and authorized operational users. This Privacy Policy explains how The BEE Suite collects, uses, discloses, protects, and retains information when users access The BEE Suite websites, applications, parent portal, mobile app, and related services.

The BEE Suite is intended for adults and authorized childcare operations users. The parent portal is not intended for child self-service accounts.

For urgent child safety, pickup, custody, billing policy, or same-day school record issues, parents and guardians should contact their childcare center directly.

## 2. Roles and Responsibilities

The BEE Suite processes information for childcare providers and related organizations that use the platform. Depending on the context, the childcare provider may control the records, retention rules, correction approvals, family authorization, and school policies that apply to the data.

The BEE Suite may act as a service provider, processor, or school service provider for a childcare provider or education organization. The BEE Suite does not sell parent, child, staff, or school data.

## 3. Information We Collect

The categories below may vary by school, enabled features, and user role.

### Account and Contact Information

We may collect names, email addresses, phone numbers, role, school, center, owner group, login credentials or authentication metadata, guardian relationship, authorized pickup status, communication preferences, support contact information, and account status.

### Child, Family, and Student Records

We may process child names, date of birth or age group, classroom, schedule, attendance, guardian relationships, emergency contacts, authorized pickups, custody or safety notes, enrollment records, registration forms, daily reports, meals, naps, diaper or potty notes, activities, supplies, incident acknowledgements, school notes, and other records entered or approved by the childcare provider.

### Health, Medical, Allergy, and Safety Information

Where enabled by a school, the platform may process allergy information, medical notes, medication records, immunization or requested documents, incident documentation, custody restrictions, pickup limitations, and other sensitive childcare records.

### Staff and Operational Records

We may process staff profiles, roles, classroom assignments, time records, certifications, compliance tasks, training records, internal messages, audit logs, and school operations records.

### Messages, Documents, Photos, and User Content

We may process parent-to-school messages, staff messages, announcements, uploaded documents, photos, message attachments, typed signatures, document review decisions, support requests, and other content submitted through the platform.

### Billing and Payment Information

We may process invoices, balances, payment status, payment history, payment method type, receipt identifiers, Stripe customer/payment identifiers, connected account information, payout status, refunds, disputes, and payment support records. The BEE Suite does not store full card numbers, full bank account numbers, or bank login credentials. Payment method entry is handled by Stripe or another approved payment provider.

### Device, Usage, Log, and Diagnostic Information

We may collect device type, browser/app version, IP address, authentication events, session metadata, page or feature usage, request metadata, security logs, audit logs, error logs, performance data, crash reports if enabled, and support diagnostics.

### Import and Integration Data

Schools may import data from prior childcare systems, spreadsheets, payment providers, messaging providers, email systems, Google services, or other integrations. Imported records may include source identifiers, raw source fields, migration metadata, and validation results.

## 4. How We Use Information

We use information to:

- Provide parent portal, staff, director, executive, billing, reporting, compliance-support, and school operations workflows.
- Authenticate users and enforce role, school, center, family, and tenant permissions.
- Let parents and guardians view linked child and family records.
- Support school communications, documents, daily reports, incident acknowledgements, billing, and payments.
- Process payments, refunds, disputes, receipts, connected account onboarding, and reconciliation.
- Provide support, troubleshoot issues, respond to requests, and improve reliability.
- Detect, investigate, and prevent abuse, fraud, unauthorized access, security incidents, and policy violations.
- Maintain audit logs for sensitive operations.
- Support imports, migrations, backups, reporting, and operational analytics.
- Comply with legal, accounting, licensing, contractual, school, and regulatory obligations.

The BEE Suite does not use child, parent, school, or staff data for third-party advertising.

## 5. How Information Is Shared

We may share information with:

- The childcare provider, school, owner group, directors, staff, and authorized users according to role and scope.
- Parents, guardians, and authorized pickup users only as permitted by school-managed family links and permissions.
- Service providers that help operate the platform, such as hosting, database, authentication, file storage, payment processing, email, SMS, analytics, crash reporting, security, and support vendors.
- Payment providers such as Stripe to process payments and related financial workflows.
- Communication providers such as SendGrid or Twilio when a school enables email or SMS workflows.
- Integration providers when a school authorizes an integration.
- Legal, safety, compliance, law enforcement, or government recipients when required or permitted by law.
- Successors in a merger, acquisition, financing, reorganization, or sale of business assets, subject to appropriate privacy protections.

We require service providers to process information only for authorized purposes and to protect it appropriately.

## 6. Children's Privacy and COPPA

The BEE Suite processes child information for childcare operations at the direction of schools, childcare providers, parents, guardians, and authorized adult users. The parent portal is not intended for child self-service accounts, and children should not create accounts or submit information directly.

When information about children under 13 is processed, The BEE Suite supports school and parent-authorized childcare operations. Schools and childcare providers are responsible for obtaining any required parent or guardian authorizations, consents, notices, and records under applicable law and school policy. The BEE Suite supports parent review, correction, and deletion request workflows subject to school approval, legal retention requirements, safety obligations, licensing rules, accounting rules, and contractual obligations.

## 7. FERPA and Education Records

Some customers may be schools or education organizations subject to FERPA or similar student privacy laws. Where applicable, The BEE Suite is intended to operate as a school service provider or school official performing services for the school under the school's control. The BEE Suite does not disclose education records except as authorized by the school, parent/guardian, applicable contract, or law.

Schools remain responsible for determining whether a record is an education record, whether FERPA applies, and how parent access, amendment, consent, and disclosure rules apply to their records.

## 8. Data Retention

We retain information for as long as needed to provide the services, maintain school records, support safety and licensing obligations, complete accounting and payment workflows, resolve disputes, enforce agreements, maintain audit logs, comply with law, and support backups and security.

Some child, family, medical, custody, attendance, billing, payment, and staff records may need to be retained by the school or The BEE Suite even after a user account is closed.

When information is no longer needed, we delete, de-identify, or archive it according to applicable retention rules and technical constraints.

## 9. Your Choices and Requests

Depending on your role and the records involved, you may be able to:

- Update account contact information.
- Change your password.
- Manage notification preferences where enabled.
- Ask your school to correct family, child, billing, or operational records.
- Request access to information associated with your account.
- Request deletion or deactivation of your account.
- Request deletion or de-identification of information where legally and operationally permitted.

Parents and guardians should usually contact their childcare center first for child, family, custody, pickup, billing policy, or school record corrections. For privacy requests, contact `support@thebeesuite.io`.

## 10. Account and Data Deletion

Users may initiate an account deletion request in the app where available or by contacting `support@thebeesuite.io`. The BEE Suite may need to verify identity, notify the relevant childcare provider, and preserve certain records required for legal, safety, licensing, accounting, audit, security, dispute, or contractual reasons.

Account deletion may remove or deactivate login access while preserving school records that the childcare provider must retain. When complete deletion is not permitted, The BEE Suite will explain the retention reason where allowed.

## 11. Security

The BEE Suite uses administrative, technical, and organizational safeguards designed to protect information. These may include encryption in transit, role-based access controls, tenant scoping, audit logs, private storage, signed file access, secure payment providers, secret management, logging redaction, and security monitoring.

No online service can guarantee absolute security. Users should protect their passwords, use trusted devices, and promptly report suspected unauthorized access.

## 12. Artificial Intelligence Features

Where enabled, The BEE Suite may provide human-reviewed draft assistance, summaries, suggestions, or operational support. AI features should not make final childcare, medical, legal, licensing, custody, safety, billing, or compliance decisions. Authorized staff remain responsible for reviewing and approving content before use.

Customer data used with AI providers must follow the customer's configuration, contracts, privacy obligations, and vendor terms.

## 13. State, Local, and Licensing Requirements

Childcare providers are responsible for complying with state licensing rules, parent notice rules, retention rules, health and safety requirements, employee record rules, and other local obligations. The BEE Suite supports operational workflows but does not provide legal, medical, licensing, custody, safety, billing, tax, or compliance advice.

## 14. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. The updated version will be posted with a revised effective date. If changes are material, we may provide additional notice through the app, email, or school communication channels.
