## Recommended V1 Direction

Start with a simple per-location SaaS subscription, then add optional revenue lines as operators adopt more of the platform:

- Base SaaS fee per location for CRM, inquiry routing, reporting, and admin workflows.
- Optional add-ons for AI, SMS/email volume, FTE reporting, automations, document/e-signature workflows, and premium support.
- Payment platform fee only when parent billing is live through Stripe Connect.
- Separate enterprise/franchise license for brand-level reporting, white-label controls, governance, and rollout support.

## Stripe Fee Positioning

The BEE Suite platform fee should stay separate from Stripe processing fees. Stripe processing is a pass-through processor cost, while The BEE Suite platform fee is the revenue line for payment facilitation, reporting, ledger workflows, and payout routing.

For launch planning, treat the bps ranges shown in the visuals as illustrative only. Final payment economics should be confirmed against Stripe pricing, expected tuition volume, support cost, state tax rules, and customer contract language.

## Decisions To Make Next

- Public v1 entry price for one location.
- Kid City USA pilot pricing and whether it is discounted during live testing.
- Whether multi-location operators pay per center, at HQ, or both.
- Which add-ons are included in the core plan versus sold separately.
- Platform fee cap or minimum fee per processed payment.
- Whether onboarding/import/setup is free, one-time paid, or included only in annual contracts.
