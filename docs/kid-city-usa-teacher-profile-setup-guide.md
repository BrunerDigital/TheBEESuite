# Kid City USA Teacher Profile Setup Guide

This guide is for teachers after their director has added them to The BEE Suite. It helps each teacher confirm their profile is active, linked to the correct school and classroom, and ready for attendance, daily reports, photos, incidents, parent messages, and staff kiosk workflows.

If anything looks wrong, ask the director or assistant director to update the teacher profile from `Teachers`. Teachers should not work from the wrong classroom because attendance, daily reports, child visibility, ratio warnings, photos, and incident reports depend on the classroom assignment.

## Before You Start

Have these items ready:

- Your BEE Suite login username or work email.
- Your temporary password if the director generated one.
- Your 4 digit staff kiosk code, if your school uses staff clock-in/out.
- Your assigned classroom name.
- Your scheduled shift.
- Any required staff documents or credential updates.

The default temporary password for generated teacher logins is `BusyBees`.

## Step 1: Log In

1. Open The BEE Suite login page.
2. Enter the username or email provided by your director.
3. Enter the temporary password.
4. If prompted, reset the password.
5. Open `Teacher portal`.

If you cannot log in, ask your director to confirm:

- Your user is active.
- Your email or generated username is spelled correctly.
- Your role is `Teacher`.
- Your staff profile is connected to the correct school.

## Step 2: Confirm Your Name, Email, And Role

1. Look at the account name shown in the app header.
2. Confirm your name is correct.
3. Confirm the email or username belongs to you.
4. Confirm your role says teacher.

Ask your director to fix the profile if:

- Your name is misspelled.
- You are using another teacher's login.
- Your account shows the wrong role.
- Your account shows the wrong school.

## Step 3: Confirm Your Classroom Assignment

1. Open `Teacher portal`.
2. Review the roster shown on the screen.
3. Confirm the children belong to your assigned classroom.
4. Look for the classroom grouping name.
5. Confirm the room name and age group match your assignment.

Your classroom assignment controls:

- Which children you can see.
- Which children you can mark present, absent, checked in, or checked out.
- Which children receive your daily reports.
- Which photos are routed for director media review.
- Which incident reports are linked to your classroom.
- Which ratio warnings include your staff coverage.
- Which parent messages you can access for classroom families.

Ask your director to update `Teachers > Quick classroom assignment` if the classroom is missing or wrong.

## Step 4: Confirm Your Roster

1. In `Teacher portal`, review every child card.
2. Confirm active children are visible.
3. Confirm children who moved rooms are no longer on your roster.
4. Confirm newly enrolled children are present.
5. Check for custody warning labels.
6. Check for allergy, medication, or safety notes your role is allowed to see.

Stop and ask a director before continuing if:

- A child is missing.
- A child from another room appears.
- Custody warnings do not match what the director told you.
- Allergy or medication details look incomplete.
- A child is visible after being withdrawn or moved.

## Step 5: Confirm Staff Kiosk Access

1. Open the school kiosk page or ask the director to open it on the check-in device.
2. Choose `Staff`.
3. Enter your work email or teacher username.
4. Enter your 4 digit staff kiosk code.
5. Verify that the kiosk shows:
   - Your name
   - Your title
   - Your classroom
   - Your current clock status
6. Test clock-in at the start of the shift.
7. Test clock-out at the end of the shift.

Ask your director to update the staff kiosk code if:

- The code does not work.
- The kiosk shows the wrong classroom.
- The kiosk shows the wrong teacher.
- Your clock status is stuck or incorrect.

## Step 6: Test Attendance

1. In `Teacher portal`, select one child.
2. Confirm the child is in your classroom.
3. Test the attendance controls with director approval.
4. Confirm the options are available:
   - Present
   - Absent
   - Sick
   - Vacation
   - Check in
   - Check out
5. Confirm saved attendance updates the child card.

Do not mark attendance for a child who is not in your classroom unless the director has assigned you temporary coverage.

## Step 7: Test Daily Reports

1. In `Teacher portal`, choose one child.
2. Confirm the daily report target is correct.
3. Add a sample care entry with director approval:
   - Meal or bottle
   - Nap
   - Diaper or potty
   - Activity
   - Mood
   - Supplies needed
   - Teacher note
4. Confirm whether `Send to parent portal` should be on or off for the test.
5. Save the report.
6. Confirm the child card shows the report status.

For normal use:

- Use `Present children` when creating reports for all children currently present.
- Use `All visible` only when the whole classroom should receive the same report details.
- Use `Selected child` for individual notes, supplies, incidents, or sensitive details.
- Review notes before sending to parents.

## Step 8: Test Photos And Media Review

1. Select a child.
2. Add a photo only if the director says the test is allowed.
3. Add a parent-facing caption.
4. Submit the photo.
5. Confirm it routes to director media review.

Photos should not be shared unless parent permissions and classroom visibility are correct. If a child has a media restriction, stop and ask the director before uploading.

## Step 9: Test Incident Reporting

1. Select the correct child.
2. Confirm the child's classroom is correct.
3. Enter the incident type.
4. Write an objective description.
5. Enter the action taken.
6. Submit the incident.
7. Confirm the director knows it is ready for review.

Incident reports require director review. Teachers should record facts, action taken, and notification details, but should not make legal, medical, custody, or compliance conclusions in the report.

## Step 10: Check Offline Queue Behavior

The teacher portal can queue classroom actions on the tablet if the connection drops.

1. Look for any offline queue message.
2. If an action is queued, keep the tablet open until the connection returns.
3. Use the sync/flush option when available.
4. Tell the director if queued actions do not sync.

Do not repeatedly enter the same attendance, incident, or daily report action if the first one was queued.

## Step 11: Confirm Communication Access

1. Open `Messages` if your role allows it.
2. Confirm you only see families connected to your assigned classroom.
3. Send messages only under the school's communication policy.
4. Review AI reply suggestions before sending.

Ask your director if:

- You see families outside your classroom.
- You cannot message a family you are responsible for.
- You are unsure whether a message should come from the teacher, director, or billing team.

## Step 12: Confirm Your Schedule And Coverage

1. Ask the director to show your schedule in `Teachers`.
2. Confirm:
   - Classroom
   - Start time
   - End time
   - Days of week
   - Status
3. Confirm ratio coverage for your room.
4. Tell the director if you are floating, covering another room, absent, late, or leaving early.

The BEE Suite ratio warnings depend on accurate staff schedules and classroom assignments.

## What Teachers Can Verify Themselves

- They can log in.
- Their name is correct.
- Their teacher portal opens.
- Their roster matches their classroom.
- Their kiosk code works.
- Their attendance controls work.
- Their daily report controls work.
- Their photo upload routes for review.
- Their incident report workflow is available.
- Offline queued actions sync when connection returns.

## What Directors Must Fix

Teachers should ask the director or assistant director to fix:

- Wrong school assignment.
- Wrong classroom assignment.
- Missing classroom.
- Missing children.
- Extra children from another classroom.
- Incorrect name, title, phone, or email.
- Inactive account.
- Wrong role.
- Missing staff kiosk code.
- Missing schedule.
- Expired or missing staff credentials.
- Incorrect custody, allergy, medication, or media permission information.
- Parent message access that is too broad or too limited.

## Teacher Launch Sign-Off

Before the classroom goes live, each teacher should confirm:

- I can log in with my own account.
- My account shows my correct name and role.
- I see the correct classroom.
- My roster is correct.
- I know which children have custody, allergy, medication, or media restrictions.
- My staff kiosk code works.
- I can clock in and clock out.
- I can mark attendance.
- I can create daily reports.
- I know when to send reports to parents.
- I know how photo review works.
- I know how to submit incidents for director review.
- I know what to do if the tablet goes offline.
- I know who to contact when my classroom, roster, schedule, or permissions are wrong.
