# Teacher Classroom Quick Start

Last updated: July 1, 2026

This guide is for teachers and classroom staff using The BEE Suite during daily classroom operations.

## Before You Start

1. Go to `https://thebeesuite.io/login`.
2. Sign in with the Bee Suite username/email and temporary password provided by your director.
3. If the app asks you to change your password, do that before entering classroom data.
4. Confirm the school and classroom shown in the portal are correct.
5. If the wrong school, classroom, or roster appears, stop and contact your director before recording attendance, photos, reports, or incidents.

## Daily Classroom Flow

Use the teacher portal as the classroom operating screen for the day:

1. Review the roster when you arrive.
2. Confirm children already checked in through the kiosk or front desk.
3. Record classroom attendance changes only for children in your assigned classroom unless a director assigned temporary coverage.
4. Watch ratio warnings and notify the director if the class is out of ratio or close to the limit.
5. Save updates throughout the day instead of waiting until pickup time.

## Attendance

- Use the attendance tools to mark classroom presence, transitions, and corrections.
- Do not check in a child who is not physically present.
- Do not check out a child unless pickup has been verified through the approved school process.
- If an attendance record looks wrong, add a note and tell the director instead of deleting or guessing.

## Daily Reports

Daily reports should be objective, parent-safe, and complete enough for families and directors to understand the day.

Include:

- Meals, bottles, snacks, naps, restroom/diapering, activities, mood, supplies needed, and notable classroom updates.
- Short, professional notes that a parent can read without extra context.
- Any director-review items that should be held before parent visibility.

Use partial save when you cannot finish the whole report immediately. Return before the end of the day to complete missing fields.

## Incidents

Create an incident report when there is an injury, behavior event, medical concern, safety concern, or director-requested documentation need.

Good incident reports include:

- Child, classroom, staff member, date/time, type, description, action taken, parent notification status, and follow-up needs.
- Objective wording: what happened, what staff observed, and what staff did.
- No blame, assumptions, diagnoses, or casual language.

If the incident is urgent, notify the director immediately. Do not rely on the report alone for urgent communication.

## Photos And Media

- Upload only school-appropriate photos/media.
- Check the child and classroom before uploading.
- Respect photo restrictions and custody/privacy notes.
- If another child appears in the background, make sure the photo is allowed before sharing.
- When unsure, mark for director review or ask before posting.

## Messages

- Keep parent messages professional, short, and factual.
- Use templates when available, then personalize if needed.
- Do not discuss another child, another family, staff discipline, confidential medical/custody details, or unapproved policy changes.
- Escalate billing, enrollment, legal, custody, licensing, or serious behavior questions to the director.

## Offline Or Poor Connectivity

- If the tablet loses connection, keep paper notes or use the approved offline/queued workflow if available.
- Do not repeatedly submit the same report or incident if the app is slow.
- When connection returns, confirm each update saved once.
- Tell the director if any record may be missing or duplicated.

## End-Of-Day Checklist

Before leaving:

- Attendance is accurate.
- Daily reports are complete or clearly saved as incomplete for director follow-up.
- Incidents are submitted and urgent items were verbally escalated.
- Photos/media are reviewed or marked for director review.
- Parent-visible notes are professional and complete.
- Supplies, classroom concerns, and follow-up tasks are communicated to the director.

## Support

When reporting an issue, include:

- School name.
- Classroom name.
- Your user email/username.
- Child name or report/incident involved, if relevant.
- Page or action attempted.
- Expected result.
- Actual result.
- Screenshot if possible.
